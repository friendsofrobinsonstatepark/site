Robinson State Park Day 2015

It's almost time again for Robinson State Park Day. In case you didn't know, the Town of Agawam has proclaimed the third Saturday of every June as Robinson State Park day. This event is celebrated in the park with a wide range of activities.

Entrance fee is $8 per car for automobiles with Massachusetts registration. All events start at the Trestle Pavilion.

This year, the schedule is as follows:

Bird Identification Walk8 am

Led by John Hutchison. Bring binoculars.

Explore the Tree Bus!9 am — 3 pm

Led by Gerri Morgan of the Wilbraham Hiking Club. Easy to moderate hiking. Poles, hiking boots, water, snack, and bug spray recommended. Appropriate for adults and older children.

Five-Mile Hike10 am

Led by Dave Conley. Easy to moderate hiking. Poles, hiking boots, water, snack, and bug spray recommended. Appropriate for adults and older children.

Nature Art Projects10 am — noon

Projects guided by Sharon Vasicek.

"Noticing Nature" walk10 am — noon

Led by Gini Traub. Appropriate for families with young children!

The CCC at Robinson State Park1:30 pm

Presentation by Gini Traub. Followed by a walk to the remains of the historical camp.

WRWA Canoe & Kayak Cruise Arrival3 pm

Watch the Westfield River Watershed Association Annual Canoe & Kayak Cruise arrive at Robinson from the Westfield River at the park’s canoe launch area.

If you have any questions, please email them to robinsonfriends@comcast.net.

Please note that severe weather or heavy rain will lead to the event being cancelled.
